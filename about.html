Background

I grew up and still live in the rural town of Kirksville, Missouri. After attending a summer camp, I became hooked on programming or anything else computer related. A few years later I found my father's old film camera in a closet. These started my love for the two creative mediums you'll find on this site: photographs and software.

If you are a recruiter, here is a more formal résumé.

Photography

For now, I'm focusing on photographing my hometown. It can be a struggle to find new things to photograph in a small, familiar town but I welcome the challenge. Photographing one place means I see it in all seasons and in all states. I have sub projects within this project - such as Through Windows or Multiplied Night. From this collections of photographs, I'm hoping to produce a book around the time I move away after university.

Some erata for the photographers who found my site: my favorite camera is an old 1980s Bronica 6x6 medium format film camera, much of my work is shot on film with this or my father's old Canon A-1 35mm SLR, I'm a sucker for some well exposed color-positive film, I'm currently trying to set up a color darkroom in my parents garage, my favorite photographers are, in no particular order: Cody Cobb, Jason Lee, Stephen Shore, and Todd Hido (these tend to change often).

You can find more of my work in a less curated fashion on Instagram.

Software

  • Spring 2019 – Attend my first hackathon, PickHacks (Missouri S&T's annumal hackathon) and write AutoChef in Python
  • Spring 2019 – Plan, organize, and attend TruHacks, Truman State University's annumal hackathon
